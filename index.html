CULTURAL FESTIVALS AROUND THE WORLD

HOLI – INDIA (Click on the image)


Every spring, people across India and around the world celebrate the Hindu festival Holi, throwing colored water and powders on one another in joyous celebration.

On this one day—the full-moon day of the Hindu month of Phalguna—societal rankings such as caste, gender, age, and status are ignored in the spirit of making merry together.

Holi's story comes from mythology: the demon king Hiranyakashipu wanted to kill his son Prahlada for worshipping Vishnu. Holika tried using fire, but she was burned and Prahlada survived. A pyre is lit to remember this story.

HALLOWEEN – USA (New York)


Halloween is a vibrant celebration on October 31st. It is rooted in the ancient Celtic festival of Samhain, marking the end of harvest and believed to blur the line between the living and the dead.
