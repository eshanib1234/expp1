CULTURAL FESTIVALS AROUND THE WORLD

HOLI – INDIA (click on the image)

Every spring, people across India and around the world celebrate the Hindu festival Holi, throwing colored water and powders on one another in joyous celebration. On this one day—the full-moon day of the Hindu month of Phalguna—societal rankings such as caste, gender, age, and status are eschewed in the spirit of making merry together. Holi’s traditions vary throughout the country and have their roots in Indian mythology. Learn more about Holi.

HALLOWEEN – USA (New York)

Halloween is a vibrant celebration observed annually on October 31st. While it's now widely recognized for its playful activities and spooky themes, Halloween has a rich history rooted in both ancient Celtic and Western Christian traditions. Halloween's origins trace back to the ancient Celtic festival of Samhain.
